### 1. Educational Purpose

This application is an educational tool developed as part of the Chicago Public Schools (CPS) Teacher Residency Program. It presents a structured social-emotional learning (SEL) framework for developing relationship skills, self-awareness, and mutual accountability in social interactions.

This application is **not** a clinical intervention, therapeutic program, psychotherapy, or mental health treatment of any kind. It does not constitute the practice of psychology, psychiatry, counseling, social work, or any other licensed clinical profession.

---

### 2. Not a Substitute for Professional Services

The framework, tools, definitions, rules of engagement, and protocols contained in this application do not replace the services of a qualified mental health professional. Users who have clinical diagnoses — including but not limited to autism spectrum disorder, post-traumatic stress disorder, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, or any other condition referenced in this framework — should receive clinical care from licensed professionals appropriate to their needs.

This application is designed to support, not supplant, a professional facilitation relationship. It is not designed for self-directed independent use without qualified educator or clinician oversight.

### 3. Crisis Situations

This application is **not** a crisis intervention tool and does not provide emergency mental health services. The emergency screen within the application provides grounding support only and does not connect users to clinical services.

If you or someone you know is experiencing a mental health crisis, suicidal ideation, or immediate danger:

- **Call or text 988** — Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (United States)
- **Call 911** — for immediate emergencies
- **Crisis Text Line** — text HOME to 741741

### 4. Scope of Practice

This application was developed by Catrina Wright in her capacity as a special education teacher and teacher residency program participant. The framework reflects evidence-informed instructional design rooted in published research in social-emotional learning, applied behavior analysis, autism education, attachment theory, and Universal Design for Learning (UDL).

Catrina Wright is not a licensed psychologist, psychiatrist, therapist, counselor, or clinical social worker. Nothing in this application should be interpreted as clinical advice, diagnostic information, or a clinical recommendation.

This framework was developed for one specific adult learner with a particular combination of learning profiles and clinical history. While the framework draws on broadly applicable research, it has not been validated as a universal instructional standard. Adaptation for other learners requires professional judgment and should involve qualified educators and clinicians familiar with the individual.

The research cited throughout this framework reflects the state of knowledge as of 2026. As with all educational and clinical research, findings evolve. References are provided for transparency and further review, not as endorsements of any particular clinical position.

### 6. AI-Generated Content

Module 4 of this application includes an AI Scenario Generator powered by Anthropic's Claude API. Generated scenarios are educational practice examples only. They do not constitute clinical assessment, diagnostic evaluation, professional advice, or therapeutic recommendation.

AI-generated content is produced by a language model and may contain inaccuracies, inappropriate framings, or content unsuitable for specific individuals. All AI-generated scenarios should be reviewed within the context of a professional facilitation relationship before use.

By using the AI Scenario Generator, users acknowledge that their situation descriptions are transmitted to Anthropic's API servers for processing. No other data is transmitted by this application. Anthropic's privacy policy governs the handling of that data: [anthropic.com/privacy](https://www.anthropic.com/privacy).

### 7. Privacy and Data

This application does not collect, store, or transmit personal data to any server or third party — with the single exception noted in Section 6 above.

All user-generated entries — including self-audit responses, journal entries, skill tracker ratings, initiation log entries, and relationship health check responses — exist only in the current browser session. This data is not saved, synced, transmitted, or accessible to anyone other than the current user. Closing the browser tab permanently deletes all session data.
